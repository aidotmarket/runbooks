## 2. Security and safety invariants

1. The age identity, Telegram token, decrypted Infisical bytes/rows and provider credentials never enter chat, a networked model, logs, argv, Git or persistent plaintext. Unattended local secrets use macOS Keychain, not `.env`.
2. Local Ollama sees only an allow-listed redacted attestation. Deterministic programs perform cryptographic and restore verification.
3. Object Lock stays COMPLIANCE; no retain-until is shortened, bypass permission added, versioning/encryption/BPA weakened, or protected version deleted.
4. Retention apply requires complete version inventory, reviewed canonical policy, separate phase-specific imminent-operation manifests and Max receipts for current-version expiration and later permanent noncurrent deletion, plus exact post-write readback. A current-expiration approval never authorizes a derived noncurrent version or clock.
5. Missing metadata, partial export, unverified delivery, ambiguous lifecycle clock, drift or malformed/qualified Council output fails closed.
6. A backup may retry one classified transient failure but may not overwrite/disguise a failed attempt.
7. Production writes/deletes and offline-key use remain blocked until their stated gates and local user action.

## 3. Zero-disclosure Infisical restore proof

### 3.1 Titan-only data path

Max keeps the age identity in 1Password; neither it nor an `op://` reference reaches Codex. Before the admitted `op read` or raw no-echo path accepts input, phase Z0 binds SHA-256, signature/build ID and canonical config/dependency/build hashes for the host input/verifier, immutable guest image/firmware/kernel/initramfs, QEMU, guest verifier, Postgres server/client, `age` and `pg_restore`; it also binds compiler/reproducible image, host OS/kernel/hypervisor, guest measurements, fixed memory and QEMU argv/FD map. Local policy rejects any mismatch, mutability, unmeasured component or unavailable verifier before input or download.

Exact topology: `op` gets only anonymous close-on-exec stdout `I_w`; the supervisor reads `I_r` directly into a prefaulted, `mlock`ed, `MADV_DONTDUMP`, registered 4,096-byte anonymous host region `H`. Alternatively it opens controlling-terminal FD `T`, disables echo/canonical editing, reads ≤256 bytes one-by-one into `H`, drains the queue, restores flags and closes `T`. After shape validation it makes one `H`→`S` copy, where `S` is one anonymous fixed 4,096-byte shared-memory FD, prefaulted/locked and size-sealed where supported, then zero-verifies `H`. QEMU alone inherits `S`, mapped by preallocated `memory-backend-file,share=on,prealloc=on` to one `ivshmem-plain` device; no pipe/socket/path/stdout/chardev exists after `S`. The guest maps that BAR into locked 4,096-byte `G`, validates fixed length/state/nonce framing, copies one identity line to a locked sealed guest memfd and gives `age` only `/proc/self/fd/N`. Ciphertext uses a separate allow-listed FD; plaintext guest pipes and all `age`/hash/`pg_restore`/Postgres pages stay within the fully pinned guest-RAM backend.

`SecretBufferMapV1` closes the proof: for each input-channel kernel page, `H`, `S`, `G`, guest memfd, registered tool scratch range and fixed VM allocation it records component/build, allocation ID/capacity/class, prefault/lock result, page ownership, residency sequence, populated interval, zeroization/zero-readback and release. A monitor begins before population, consumes allocation/lock/unlock/fault/process events and samples every range at a declared maximum interval. Missing/gapped evidence, post-population allocation, unregistered copy, unlocked/nonresident page, overrun/unbounded allocation, monitor loss, sleep/power event or early unlock aborts. The exact `op` build and `I` channel must attest all internal identity allocations/kernel pages; the reader/platform must do the same for `T`. Stock `op`, TTY, macOS, QEMU, `age` or kernel behavior is not assumed sufficient: `mlock`, disabled swap or one residency sample alone is not proof. An unobservable/unlockable process, driver or channel page refuses that path before secret input. R16 claims only this evidence-backed refusal, not that Titan already has every capability.

After guest consumption, `H`, `S`, `G`, BAR and guest memfd are `explicit_bzero`ed with barriers and zero-readback; all registered scratch ranges then zeroize in dependency order before unlock/unmap and VM destruction. Synthetic canaries scan outside registered maps plus kernel/channel audit surfaces, FDs, argv/environment, logs/files, swap/core/crash/hibernation and post-teardown RAM/channel probes. Inaccessible/incomplete scans or residue fail. Only allocation/count/capacity, event/sample/zero-page roots and PASS/FAIL leave the verifier. A wholly guest-local alternative remains outside scope pending exact review.

Under that capability gate, the deterministic verifier:

1. enumerates `postgres/infisical/*.dump.age` newest-to-oldest, selects the newest complete valid producer-signed version, and records nonsecret S3 provenance/metadata;
2. downloads ciphertext only into a mode-0700 temporary directory;
3. receives exactly one raw `AGE-SECRET-KEY-...` line through the admitted input component into `H`, performs exactly one bounded `H`→`S` copy, proves `H` zeroization, and permits exactly one guest `S`→`G` consumption under the nonce/state protocol;
4. passes it only through the locked guest memfd to `age`; decrypted bytes flow through guest anonymous pipes, whose pages remain inside pinned guest RAM, to hashing and `pg_restore`;
5. restores inside a dedicated ephemeral QEMU VM running the exact Postgres major bound by the producer-signed manifest and attested source-production major (currently 18 for Infisical), with guest swap absent, runtime/data on guest tmpfs, an immutable read-only OS image, no writable block device and `network=none`;
6. verifies the producer-signed manifest and compares its plaintext/ciphertext SHA-256, bytes, TOC and transaction identity before checking the reviewed critical-schema and semantic-assertion manifests;
7. emits only a mode-0600 redacted attestation;
8. in `finally`, terminates the complete QEMU process group, waits and reaps every child, closes every inherited/monitor/serial/secret FD and chardev, unmounts and verifies teardown of every guest tmpfs/shared mount, removes downloaded ciphertext and ephemeral directories, and attests no residual process, FD, mount, socket, chardev, firmware/NVRAM state, tmpfs allocation or file remains.

The Titan parent runs with `RLIMIT_CORE=0` and launches QEMU with `-overcommit mem-lock=on` (not lazy/on-fault) after proving the hard memlock limit exceeds the full fixed VM allocation, QEMU overhead and every registered host/shared range. The VM may start only when QEMU and the independent monitor confirm that all guest/host-emulator memory and `S` are preallocated and pinned; any unregistered or unlocked page refuses the run. QEMU uses `dump-guest-core=off`, the phase-Z0-hashed immutable firmware/image, no writable pflash/NVRAM/firmware-variable store, no path-backed writable chardev/monitor/serial socket, and no writable QEMU/debug/log path. The only secret channel is the exact `S`/ivshmem topology above. Guest boot verifies the attested image measurements, `SwapTotal=0`, no swap device/file, no writable block device, no crash dump/kdump, tmpfs for every runtime/data/log path, and network disabled. The attested host profile proves host swap disabled rather than merely unused, sleep/hibernation and hibernation-image persistence disabled, and kernel core/crash dumps disabled before secret retrieval; any state change, sleep/power transition or monitor/residency failure aborts and invalidates the run.

For an admitted run, every registered identity-bearing host page and every plaintext-bearing guest userspace/kernel page is accounted for by `SecretBufferMapV1`; guest plaintext stays inside the full guest-RAM backend whose lock and residency are continuously evidenced. Only ciphertext and the immutable secret-free VM/firmware images touch persistent storage. Exact environment and FD allow-lists prevent inheritance by AWS, QEMU management, cleanup or Ollama. No database password appears in argv/environment. If the closed page/accounting claim cannot be evidenced for the exact build, the verifier reports a fixed capability refusal and does not retrieve the identity.

Each process group has a deadline. Timeout, signal, partial copy, framing/nonce failure, guest close or interrupted `op read`/terminal input closes FDs, terminates/reaps children and executes the registered zeroization DAG. A manifest/source-production Postgres-major mismatch or use of a different-major restore client refuses same-major PASS. A separately reported optional next-major compatibility drill may detect upgrade risk, but never substitutes for or weakens the exact same-major proof. Any unavailable tool/profile/key/QEMU/platform-page capability, invalid identity shape, preflight hash/measurement mismatch, metadata/hash/TOC/schema mismatch, host or guest memlock/swap/sleep preflight failure, tmpfs/read-only-disk/network failure, buffer-map/residency/FD control failure, restore failure or incomplete cleanup refuses PASS.

Raw stdout/stderr, verbose/debug tracing, shell traces, core/container logs and environment/command dumps are forbidden. Required catalog output is bounded and parsed in memory, rejected for forbidden content, reduced to allow-listed counts/digests and zeroed. Child errors become fixed nonsecret failure class plus numeric status. Before exit, leak probes scan child argv/environment/FDs, temporary paths, mounts/log settings and the audit payload; inability to prove no secret/restored-data persistence is failure.

Every backup is accompanied by a canonical producer-signed manifest issued through an isolated signing authority. Its signing key is unavailable to the backup writer/uploader and is anchored by a separately controlled, pinned verification key plus an immutable append-only audit record. It binds manifest schema/version and unique nonce/sequence; source/service; bucket/key/version ID; ciphertext and plaintext digests/bytes; production time; for Postgres, the source server major read from the live producer connection, backup-client major and LSN/transaction; or Qdrant snapshot identity; TOC/snapshot and schema-manifest versions; and producer result. Verification rejects unsigned, unknown-key, revoked-key, replayed/duplicate-sequence, stale, mismatched or result-failed objects. Restore selection walks newest to oldest and chooses the newest complete valid signed recovery point, never merely the newest object. Object Lock/versioning preserves the exact signed version but does not substitute for this producer authenticity.

The signed manifest also binds a versioned non-disclosing semantic assertion set. In the isolated guest it verifies allow-listed row-count bounds and keyed canonical digests, enabled/validated constraints, exact migration head, presence/counts of critical record classes, and isolated read-only functional checks. Assertion keys are separately controlled; only assertion IDs, expected/observed equality or bounded counts and PASS/FAIL leave the guest—never rows, values, secret names or raw query output. Any assertion/version/manifest mismatch fails the restore point.

### 3.2 Attestation and local AI

The allow-listed attestation records schema/time/host/profile; the complete phase-Z0 executable/config/build/hash allow-list and guest image/firmware/PCR measurements; bucket/key/version/last-modified/ciphertext bytes; signed-manifest/key/audit/nonce identity; expected/observed plaintext hash/bytes/TOC; semantic-assertion-set identity/results; producer-manifest/source-production/guest-server/client Postgres majors and exact equality; restore/catalog counts; fixed QEMU argv/FD/device topology and memory; `SecretBufferMapV1` allocation/event/sample/zeroization roots and maximum sample gap; full-VM and every admitted userspace/kernel/channel page lock/residency outcome; host swap/hibernation-image/kernel-dump state; guest `SwapTotal=0`; guest-core-dump-off; absent writable block/firmware/NVRAM/chardev/log/crash-dump paths; tmpfs/network/sleep controls; RLIMIT/interruption/process-group-reaping/mount/ciphertext/residual-resource cleanup outcomes; platform capability verdict; overall PASS; and RFC 8785 self-hash. Hashing canonicalizes with existing `attestation_sha256=null`; independent verification repeats this and constant-time compares. Capability refusal records only the failed capability ID and phase-Z0 hashes, never secret input.

It excludes rows, secret names, 1Password reference, private-material fingerprints, environment data and raw child output; any unknown field or forbidden-pattern match fails. Only after deterministic PASS may optional `llama3.3:70b` at `127.0.0.1` review this attestation/rubric. It cannot override failure.

## 4. Telegram cutover and fail-loud delivery

Max confirms the rotated token is recoverable from an approved secure source, then enters token/chat ID directly into Apple's `security add-generic-password -w` prompts for versioned Keychain items; values never enter installer argv/history. The installer reads only candidate items, validates locally, calls `getMe`, and sends `[BACKUP CANARY - NO ACTION REQUIRED]` plus session/task ID. It requires HTTP 2xx, JSON `ok=true`, numeric `message_id`, then atomically switches a nonsecret mode-0600 pointer.

Before `.env` change, it inventories repositories, LaunchAgents and running configurations referencing either Telegram variable. Stale lines in `/Users/max/koskadeux-mcp/.env` are removed without plaintext backup only after all consumers are migrated and the Keychain-backed watchdog passes a second canary. No unrelated service restarts.

For the full 24 hours after pointer cutover, rollback by pointer reversal to intact superseded items remains valid. A mode-0600 one-shot user LaunchAgent (fixed helper plus nonsecret item names only) becomes eligible only at the later of `(second canary PASS)` and `(cutover + 24 hours)`, and must delete the named superseded items within one hour after that eligibility time. It may never delete them earlier. It writes a nonsecret receipt to both audit stores and disables itself; the watchdog alerts on missing/failed/early/late cleanup. Receipt fields: cutover, second-canary PASS/message ID, calculated eligibility, schedule/attempt/completion, UID, item names, outcome and label. The disabled plist remains until next reviewed cleanup or 90 days, then is removed only after both receipts. After deletion, recovery is local re-entry of the same rotated token, not provider re-rotation.

### 4.2 Secret-safe Telegram sender

A reviewed in-process HTTPS client—not `curl` or any child—reads Keychain values into locked memory, constructs the endpoint internally, disables inherited proxies/debug tracing, verifies TLS, limits one attempt to a five-second connect/read budget, accepts only allow-listed nonsecret message fields, parses bounded JSON in memory, and zeroes response/credential buffers. Token-bearing URL, request/body, token, chat ID, Keychain output and raw response/exception never reach argv, environment, process lists, crash reports, stdout/stderr or logs.

Success requires `ok=true` and numeric message ID. Logs contain only fixed error class, HTTP status and success message ID. Missing credentials, TLS/timeout/HTTP/parse/API failure exits nonzero. Exceptions are normalized after forbidden-content scanning. Tests force every failure/signal/crash path and probe process/audit artifacts for leakage.

## 5. Retention and storage reduction

### 5.1 Classification and Object Lock

Future uploads use collision-resistant unique keys and refuse overwrite. The writer cannot tag or change retention. New objects remain untagged and match no expiration rule until one daily controller assigns a version-specific inventory-derived classification tag (`retention_class=daily|monthly|forensic`) to an exact declared family. No covered family—including Git mirrors, AIM Data, Titan/vectorAIz exports or a later source—can exist outside the authoritative inventory and envelope. Forensic admission requires its bounded class/disposition and never advances a recovery point.

The bucket's default 35-day COMPLIANCE retention remains mandatory for every new data object. Classification never shortens it; selected monthly versions are extended from classification/promotion time to 400 days before tagging.

The first integrity-verified UTC object per family/month becomes `retention_class=monthly` only after its exact version receives a 400-day COMPLIANCE extension; other data versions become `retention_class=daily`. Integrity requires exact producer `status=ok`, matching bytes, required SHA/TOC/snapshot metadata, noncritical anomaly state and, for topology/Cloudflare, schema completeness. Listing/nonzero upload alone is insufficient.

Existing classification uses fully paginated `ListObjectVersions`, including every current/noncurrent version and delete marker. Each row records identity/state/bytes/dates, inferred noncurrent-since from the immediately newer entry, lock, version tags, integrity evidence and eligibility. Ambiguous clocks remain untagged/ineligible. Delete markers get explicit disposition and are never recovery points.

Classification alone never makes a version lifecycle-eligible. The current-expiration rule requires both `retention_class` and the version-specific `current_expiry_approved=true` tag; the permanent-noncurrent-deletion rule instead requires `retention_class` and the independently version-specific `noncurrent_deletion_approved=true` tag. Only the exact phase-approved apply path may set either tag. The two tags are not aliases and one never satisfies the other's rule. Uncertainty fails toward retention and alerts. Daily reconciliation requires one class tag per data version and a verified 400-day monthly point per completed family/month, but it cannot write either approval tag. On UTC day 3, missing monthly is critical. A corrupt monthly is never unlocked/downgraded: promote/lock the next verified candidate and receipt it; if none exists, remain critical until a replacement restore passes.

### 5.2 Fenced mutation broker

All controller/operator mutations share a 15-minute optimistic-CAS Living State lease, renewed by minute 10; each acquisition increments a monotonic fence. Controllers have no S3 mutation credential. One broker owns restricted prefix list/version-list, version-tag get/put and Object Lock get/put only—no object read/delete/bypass/lifecycle/invariant-changing permission. Its tag policy permits only `retention_class`, `current_expiry_approved` and `noncurrent_deletion_approved` on the exact inventory-derived object-version ARN and denies approval-tag removal or value changes except through a new exact-approved journal operation. Its retention policy permits only 399–401 days from trusted classification/promotion time.

The separate short-lived installer has one write, `s3:PutLifecycleConfiguration`, on the exact inventory bucket ARN, conditioned on reviewed policy hash plus existing `aimarket:purpose=backups`/environment resource tags. Reads are exactly `s3:GetLifecycleConfiguration|s3:GetBucketVersioning|s3:GetObjectLockConfiguration|s3:GetEncryptionConfiguration|s3:GetBucketPublicAccessBlock|s3:GetBucketTagging`; there is no object/version ARN or tag/session-tag write. Explicit deny: `s3:PutObjectLockConfiguration|s3:PutBucketVersioning|s3:PutEncryptionConfiguration|s3:DeleteBucketEncryption|s3:PutBucketPublicAccessBlock|s3:DeletePublicAccessBlock|s3:PutBucketTagging|s3:DeleteBucketTagging|s3:PutObjectTagging|s3:DeleteObjectTagging|s3:PutObjectRetention|s3:BypassGovernanceRetention|s3:DeleteObject|s3:DeleteObjectVersion|s3:PutBucketPolicy|s3:DeleteBucketPolicy|s3:PutBucketAcl|s3:PutObjectAcl`. Invariants/tags/protected versions are read-only. Unsupported conditions or another needed write blocks for fresh review; access never widens.

Before each write, the broker persists a `prepared` row with operation/fence/owner, exact key/version, target, plan/manifest hashes and expected pre/post-state; it rereads current lease, then writes, exact-readbacks and records `applied` before another operation. Timeout/lost response records `indeterminate` and blocks that family/version.

Indeterminate reconciliation is append-only: exact target → `applied`; exact recorded pre-state → `reconciled_not_applied` (a new fence may prepare the same target); any third state stays blocked. An expired lease with nonterminal rows cannot auto-replace. Replacement proves prior broker termination, reconciles rows, then obtains a new fence. Old holders cannot reach S3; broker serializes fences. Any lease/journal/readback failure stops before the next write and alerts.

### 5.3 Lifecycle and exact approval

Family-prefix/tag-scoped canonical rules use disjoint phase approval tags:

- daily current expiry after 35 days requires `retention_class=daily` AND `current_expiry_approved=true` and may create only a delete marker;
- monthly current expiry after 400 days requires `retention_class=monthly` AND `current_expiry_approved=true` and may create only a delete marker;
- daily permanent noncurrent removal one day after the now-observed noncurrent clock requires `retention_class=daily` AND `noncurrent_deletion_approved=true`;
- monthly permanent noncurrent removal one day after the now-observed noncurrent clock requires `retention_class=monthly` AND `noncurrent_deletion_approved=true`;
- no automatic delete-marker cleanup; markers remain explicitly inventoried/retained because an untagged marker rule could bypass exact approval;
- no unknown, unclassified or phase-unapproved action.

The second tags never cross-match. Object Lock wins. Current eligibility uses exact creation/class clock and 35/400-day class; permanent eligibility exists only after a complete post-transition inventory proves the exact version noncurrent and fixes its noncurrent-since clock. Ambiguity retains; marker cleanup remains outside scope pending separate exact-version review.

Read-only plan emits immutable desired state plus two canonical projections. Phase A contains exact current `CURRENT_EXPIRY_DELETE_MARKER` rows and current survivors; phase B contains only proven-noncurrent `PERMANENT_NONCURRENT_DELETE` rows, clocks and survivors. Both type markers/context non-operatively and track journal-backed DAG position.

Max signs a separate receipt per projection, binding desired/projection SHA-256, `A_CURRENT_EXPIRY|B_NONCURRENT_DELETE`, approver/time/expiry/totals. A row binds receipt/bucket/key/version/class/exact clock(s)/operation. Phase A permits only `current_expiry_approved=true`, contains no derived noncurrent identity/clock and cannot authorize permanent deletion. Its marker event is only a hint; a fresh complete inventory must prove the prior version noncurrent, record the marker and fix noncurrent-since. Only a new phase-B plan/receipt may then permit `noncurrent_deletion_approved=true`; already-proven noncurrent versions may enter B directly, never via A. Cross-phase use fails even if key/version/class match.

Each projection types rows as operative, `RETAINED`, oldest-`SURVIVOR`, `MARKER`, `AWAITING_TRANSITION` or `CONTEXT`. Apply fences, rereads complete inventory/read-only invariants, reproduces hashes, extends monthly locks/reconciles class tags, and writes one approval tag only on exact receipt/key/version/class/phase/clock/operation equality. Any other/absent/duplicate/mismatched row is untaggable.

On partial failure, the receipt records fence, artifact/desired-state hashes, completed DAG prefix, next step, every exact operation/readback, failure, lifecycle state, release/retry and operator/session. Retry proves prior termination, reconciles nonterminal rows, rereads all state and reconstructs original desired state plus completed prefix. It rejects unjournaled new/missing identities, tag/lock/invariant/lifecycle/eligibility drift, reordered/nonprefix completion, unexplained satisfied state or changed deletion disposition.

Retry regenerates the phase manifest. Any late/concurrent identity, transition, derived clock, marker or eligibility delta stops writes and requires a fresh phase approval; stored A hashes never authorize B.

Lifecycle enablement is the irreversible boundary. Before it, apply freezes or drains queued classifier/broker work at a recorded DAG boundary, stages the exact lifecycle rules as `Disabled`, reads back byte-equivalent canonical JSON, and proves expiry/noncurrent handling, marker survival and Object Lock behavior in a disposable versioned Object-Locked bucket. Drift, queued arrival, failed readback/proof or an indeterminate journal row stops and returns to read-only planning. Pre-enable rollback removes/restores only the disabled staged rules and reverses non-monotonic planning state; retention extensions remain safe monotonic writes. After a final fresh exact-artifact Council approval and Max approval immediately before the enable call, the broker rechecks receipt/inventory/queue equality and enables/readbacks the same rules. After enable, rollback cannot promise recovery of an asynchronously deleted version: stop by disabling the rules, preserve and inventory retained survivors, and restore/reupload a verified survivor as a new Object-Locked version under a fresh plan. No queued work resumes until post-boundary reconciliation. The approved 35/400-day periods do not change.

After install, classification cannot create eligibility. Each later current batch needs fresh complete inventory, phase-A projection and exact Max approval; after transition it stays permanently ineligible until another complete inventory, phase-B projection and new exact approval. Any identity/marker/clock delta aborts; exact row equality, not receipt membership, governs.

Lifecycle observations use the official durable direct S3 service-event path: an EventBridge rule accepts only `Object Deleted` events for this bucket with `reason=Lifecycle Expiration` and `deletion-type` in `Delete Marker Created|Permanently Deleted`, targets an encrypted Standard SQS hint queue, and has a separate encrypted target DLQ for delivery exhaustion. The consumer is idempotent by AWS event ID plus bucket/key/version/reason/deletion type/sequencer, runs at least once per minute, and redrives poison messages to a processing DLQ after five receives. Both DLQs, any oldest-visible hint over five minutes, any target-delivery failure and any consumer gap over five minutes alert outside this path and freeze lifecycle mutations. EventBridge/SQS is a low-latency hint path, not a completeness or ordering oracle. This relies only on current official AWS behavior: direct S3 service events to EventBridge are classified `Durable`; lifecycle `Object Deleted` events distinguish marker creation from permanent deletion and include version ID; notifications can duplicate or reorder; and `ListObjectVersions` requires both continuation markers and may return invalid XML with HTTP 200.

A fully paginated authoritative `ListObjectVersions` reconciliation starts every six hours, pins expected bucket owner, uses no delimiter, includes both `Versions` and `DeleteMarkers`, and follows both `NextKeyMarker` and `NextVersionIdMarker` until `IsTruncated=false`. It must finish within 30 minutes, giving a maximum authoritative observation latency of 6 hours 30 minutes from a disappearance. Invalid XML despite HTTP 200, cursor repetition/regression, a partial page set, missed start by over five minutes, auth/owner mismatch or deadline failure invalidates the poll, alerts independently and freezes mutation. Each complete snapshot and page-chain hash is immutable; comparison is only complete-to-complete.

The append-only lifecycle ledger records the exact phase approval receipt/operative-row hash and tag readback, every raw event-hint hash and disposition, both complete inventory roots, and the reconciled transition/permanent-deletion result. A `Permanently Deleted` hint is accepted only when bucket/key/version ID maps uniquely to the still-active canonical phase-B row on receipt ID/key/version/class/noncurrent clock/deletion clock/operation=`PERMANENT_NONCURRENT_DELETE`; otherwise it is quarantined, alerts immediately and freezes mutations. A `Delete Marker Created` hint is only a phase-A transition hint: it never proves or authorizes permanent deletion and is linked to an exact approved phase-A row only after the next complete inventory proves the prior exact version became noncurrent and the new marker is explicitly inventoried. That transition closes phase A and creates only input for a later phase-B plan. Inventory is authoritative: an event with a still-present version remains `EVENT_UNCONFIRMED`; an inventory disappearance with no event becomes `INVENTORY_ONLY`, is matched to the exact phase-B row, and raises a lost/delayed-event alert without hiding the deletion. An out-of-manifest, cross-phase, ambiguous, wrong-version/class/clock/receipt, retained/survivor/marker/awaiting-transition/context disappearance is critical and freezes mutations. Reconciliation also alerts on overdue approved operations and recovery-data bytes/objects or growth outside each finite steady-state envelope. Async deletion is irreversible; disabling rules cannot recover an expired version.

Markers remain explicitly inventoried and protected, but their count is not called a perpetual steady state. Recovery-data current/noncurrent bytes and objects have finite 35/400-day steady-state envelopes; marker count has a separate finite planning horizon from lifecycle enablement through `enablement + 400 days`. Gate 4 binds the exact current marker count, horizon start/end, per-family maximum expected current-version expirations and their summed worst-case new-marker count. Any observed count or creation rate above that bound, unexplained negative delta, unknown marker or mismatch to an approved transition alerts and freezes mutations. Automatic marker cleanup stays disabled. Before the horizon expires, a separate exact-version marker-cleanup design must receive fresh exact review and Max approval; absent that approval by horizon-minus-30-days, no new deletion approvals may be tagged and the condition remains critical rather than silently extending or claiming a forever bound.

`backup-health/`, `RESTORE-README.md`, audit evidence and unknown prefixes are untouched.

### 5.4 Quantitative reduction proof

Baseline `I0` is the last complete `ListObjectVersions` snapshot before any approval tag; post-state is the first stabilized pair `I1a/I1b`. The 24 July 322-current-object / 257,083,842,321-current-byte audit is only a lower-bound anchor because it omitted noncurrent versions/markers; `I0` must reconcile every later upload/transition/deletion and cannot be lower without exact ledger rows. Per family and total, each snapshot gives exact identities plus current bytes/count, noncurrent bytes/count, marker count and marker data bytes (canonical zero; estimated metadata charge stays outside), lock/tag/class/age, pre/post-cleanup main-Postgres cohort and post-cleanup P50/P95 upload size.

Stabilization requires all A/B rows terminal, no pending approved operation/conflict, ≥72 hours since last permanent deletion, and two complete inventories ≥24 hours apart with zero unexplained delta. Uploads remain allowed/ledgered; marker/unapproved deletion remains disabled. Per family, `U` is new current versions/bytes, `T` approved current→noncurrent transitions, `D` phase-B-bound permanent deletions and `M` created markers:

- `I1.current_bytes = I0.current_bytes + U.bytes - T.bytes`;
- `I1.noncurrent_bytes = I0.noncurrent_bytes + T.bytes - D.bytes`;
- `I1.current_count = I0.current_count + U.count - T.count`;
- `I1.noncurrent_count = I0.noncurrent_count + T.count - D.count`;
- `I1.marker_count = I0.marker_count + M.count` and `I1.marker_data_bytes = 0`;
- normalized byte reduction `R.bytes = (I0.current_bytes + I0.noncurrent_bytes) - ((I1.current_bytes + I1.noncurrent_bytes) - U.bytes) = D.bytes`;
- normalized version reduction `R.count = (I0.current_count + I0.noncurrent_count) - ((I1.current_count + I1.noncurrent_count) - U.count) = D.count`.

Identity/count/byte tolerance is zero; report lag gets no allowance. `D` must exactly sum distinct phase-B rows and disappeared versions, with no duplicate, unattributed or A-only row. The per-family 35/400-day projection lists exact retained current/noncurrent/monthly versions, P95 scheduled uploads, lock horizon, oldest survivor, expected A transitions and markers through 400 days. Only the forecast permits one scheduled object/family and 5% bytes; equations stay exact and excess blocks pending fresh reviewed explanation.

Gate 4 requires normalized `I1` bytes ≤80% of `I0`, `R.bytes≥50 GiB`, and `R.count≥10%` of `I0` data-version count. Every B-approved eligible legacy main-Postgres version above post-cleanup P95 is gone; any remainder is an exact projected 35/400-day survivor. This never authorizes protected/unapproved deletion. If locks/policy prevent the target, Gate 4 fails for fresh review; finite envelope, current-only baseline or ambiguous/unattributed delta never passes.

## 6. Backup hardening and Qdrant decision

### 6.1 Postgres, Qdrant and Cloudflare

Postgres gets one retry only for classified reset/server-closed/transient network failures, with bounded jitter/backoff, fresh temp path and recorded first failure. Auth/permission/disk/encryption/validation/upload/signing failures do not retry. Publication requires the producer-signed manifest contract in section 3.1, immutable audit anchoring, size/SHA-256, `pg_restore --list`, semantic-assertion identity and successful result. Qdrant and configuration producers use the same source-appropriate signed fields. No unsigned or mismatched artifact advances freshness, monthly selection or restore eligibility.

Each source has immutable baseline epoch in `stable/reset_pending/collecting/critical`. Stable uses seven verified noncritical artifacts. Cleanup/reindex reset receipt—stored before run—binds old epoch/median/count, reason/change, approved expected range, Max/Council approval, one-run default and ≤72-hour expiry. First in-range structural success starts a new collecting epoch; old samples never mix. Under seven samples, enforce approved range and, at ≥3, current median; seven establishes stable. Expiry is critical and needs new approval.

Normal anomaly gates warn below 60%/above 180% of seven-run median and are critical below 20%/above 500%. Critical/out-of-range artifacts go only to forensic `status=failed`, untagged, with no freshness/monthly advancement, and alert. Warning artifacts cannot be monthly without explicit integrity acceptance.

Qdrant reports each required collection independently with bytes/hash, cluster/version and per-collection anomaly; no small collection masks `knowledge_base_v2`. One transient snapshot/download/upload retry is allowed; auth/schema/hash/missing collection is not. Cloudflare requires DNS/zone settings for both zones, schema-complete exports and explicit required/regenerable KV classification. HTTP errors/missing sections fail; forensic uploads do not advance freshness.

### 6.2 Qdrant cadence gate

Daily 35-day snapshots remain until a measured rebuild of `knowledge_base_v2` from exact restored Postgres completes inside four-hour full-platform RTO. Evidence binds Postgres object/version and transaction/LSN, `qdrant_sync_outbox` high-water, Qdrant cluster/version and rebuild commit, and proves Postgres plus retained events/outbox authoritative.

Nondeterministic index bytes are rebuilt/validated separately. Effective data RPO from authoritative Postgres/outbox must be ≤24 hours.

Only then may `knowledge_base_v2` full snapshots become weekly (seven-day snapshot RPO while effective data RPO remains 24 hours). ## 7. Independent monitoring and recovery objectives
